We are here to support and keep our community safe. We must stress the importance of staying at home, only leaving when absolutely necessary. RED CARD TO REQUEST HELP

Firstly, and probably most important of all, we want to look after our neighbours. Your neighbours and our volunteers will be checking on everyone to make sure we are all safe. If everything is okay (you don't have to, but could put something green at your window); BUT if you need help, please put something RED against your window where people can see and someone will come to check on you. Anything red, (tea towel, t-shirt etc) will let us know, you need support (If you feel comfortable, also write your phone number in the window so that someone can call you). If we all do this, we can all help each other through these 'unprecedented times'. If you see a RED signal but are unable to help, please call 07795071741. We can deliver a red card through your door if required. Please call the Clerk. Thank you and Keep Safe.

Practical Advice - for those feeling unwell

IN AN EMERGENCY - where the Defibrillator is required. It is positioned on the side of the Village Hall on Main Street, LS24 9RF. Should you need it in an emergency, the code is C147.

Prescription Delivery Service Support

We would suggest you contact your Pharmacy or Medical Practice directly for your prescription. If ou need support to collect medications, please phone the numbers above.

If you use APPS, the following 2 Apps are very useful:

Good SAM ALERTER - for non Covid-19 emergencies

Developed by the London Ambulance Service and London Hospital, this is a global app to alert a Medic who may be located near you.

Covid-19 join Zoe.com – COVID-19 Symptom Tracker App

Set up by King’s College, Guys and St Thomas’s Hospital. Lets anyone healthy or with symptoms share valuable information. You can report your health status daily.
